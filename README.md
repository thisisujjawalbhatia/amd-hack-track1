This repository contains the implementation for the **AMD AI Premier League (AAIPL)**, an elite competitive track of the AMD AI Reinforcement Learning Hackathon hosted at **IIT Delhi (Yardi School of AI)**. 

---

## 📖 Tournament Overview
The competition follows a **1v1 knockout format** structured like a cricket match. Two teams compete across two "innings," swapping roles between the Questioner and the Answerer.

### The Match Logic:
* **1st Inning:** Team A (Q-Agent) generates $N$ questions $\rightarrow$ Team B (A-Agent) answers.
* **2nd Inning:** Team B (Q-Agent) generates $N$ questions $\rightarrow$ Team A (A-Agent) answers.
* **Final Score:** Combined efficiency of your Q-Agent's ability to stump opponents and your A-Agent's accuracy.

---

## 🤖 The Dual-Agent System

### 1. Q-Agent (The Pitcher)
Tasked with generating $N$ puzzle-based questions across specific domains.
* **Focus Areas:** Logical Reasoning (Syllogisms), Puzzles (Seating Arrangements), Blood Relations, and Alphanumeric Series.
* **Constraint:** Must output valid JSON containing `topic`, `question`, `choices`, `answer`, and `explanation`.
* **SLA:** Must generate each question in **< 13 seconds**.

### 2. A-Agent (The Batter)
Tasked with solving adversarial questions generated by opposing teams.
* **Input:** Receives `question` and `choices`.
* **Output:** Must provide the `answer` (letter) and a brief `reasoning` (max 100 words).
* **SLA:** Must generate each answer in **< 9 seconds**.

## 🏗 Technical Methodology & Optimization



### Hardware-Software Synergy
Developed on **AMD Instinct™ MI300X** accelerators using **ROCm 6.x**, we optimized the base **Qwen3-4B** model using:
* **Unsloth:** For 2x faster fine-tuning kernels.
* **GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization):** To align agent behavior with competitive reward functions.
* **Synthetic Data Generation:** Creating adversarial puzzle datasets to improve zero-shot deductive reasoning.

---

## 🏅 Evaluation & Scoring

Scores are calculated based on competitive accuracy:

$$\text{A-agent Score} = \frac{\# \text{ of questions correctly answered}}{N} \times 100$$
$$\text{Q-agent Score} = \frac{\# \text{ of questions incorrectly answered by opponent}}{N} \times 100$$

> **⚠️ Disqualification Rule:** Teams must maintain a **50% format-correctness rate**. Failure to generate valid JSON for at least half of the requested questions leads to automatic disqualification.
